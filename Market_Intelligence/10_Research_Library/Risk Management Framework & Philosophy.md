## 📌 Key Insights

### **1. Defense Over Offense**

- Great trading is about **playing defense**, not offense.
- Assume every position is wrong at the open; know your stops and max drawdown before trading.

### **2. Risk = Losing Money**

- The author’s definition: **If your P&L is red, you are wrong**.
- Large drawdowns (e.g., 50%) are not “normal”—they indicate a failure to cut losses early.

### **3. Use Stops Always (USA)**

- Every position must have a **predetermined stop** before entry.
- Stops are non-negotiable and honored without exception—this requires extreme discipline.

### **4. Process Over Outcome**

- A day with **50 losing trades but perfect process execution is a good day**.
- Separate **process** from **outcome**; luck and skill must be parsed clearly.

### **5. Self-Distrust as Strength**

- The best traders know their minds are weak and **distrust their future selves**—especially in a crisis.
- This leads to **preventive measures** (like hard stops) rather than reactive emotional decisions.

### **6. Discipline, Humility, Patience**

- These are the **three pillars** of trading and life success.
- Humility means accepting the market is always right; discipline means consistent self-control.

### **7. Strength Through Admission of Weakness**

- Acknowledging your weaknesses (e.g., emotional bias, ego) allows you to build **robust systems**.
- Paradoxically, **true strength comes from vulnerability and self-awareness**.

### **8. Life and Trading Are Connected**

- The same principles of **self-mastery, discipline, and humility** apply to both trading and personal life.
- Conformity and ego are enemies; **self-discipline is a daily war** that leads to peace and clarity.

### **9. Identifying Winners vs. Losers**

- Winners help others; losers mock them.
- Disciplined people **never criticize others’ lack of control**—they understand the struggle firsthand.

### **10. No One-Size-Fits-All**

> - The right framework is what works **for you**, not what works for Buffett, Druckenmiller, or Simons.

- It takes **years of mistakes and experience** to find your own system
