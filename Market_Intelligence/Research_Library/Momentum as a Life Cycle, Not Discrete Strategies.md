The article argues that momentum trading is best understood as a **continuous life cycle** rather than a collection of separate strategies. While different traders may appear to use distinct setups, they are actually participating in different phases of the same underlying momentum process.

The author references several well-known momentum traders (Boucher, Qullamaggie, Landry, etc.) to illustrate that their methods differ mainly in **entry timing**, not in core structure. The momentum cycle begins with a period of accumulation and ends with exhaustion, passing through several predictable phases along the way.

### 🔄 **The Six Phases of the Momentum Life Cycle**

#### 1. **Precondition: Long Basing / Accumulation**
- **What happens:** Volatility compresses, price trades sideways, weak holders exit.
- **Why it matters:** This phase builds potential energy for the next move. It’s a _necessary condition_ for momentum.

#### 2. **Initiation: Expansion Out of the Base**
- **What happens:** First breakout—often a gap, high-volume thrust bar, or sharp range expansion.
- **Key trait:** High asymmetry (potential reward) but also high uncertainty.

#### 3. **Confirmation: Day 2 Follow-Through**
- **What happens:** Continuation after the initial breakout.
- **Key trait:** Lower failure risk, higher confidence, slightly reduced upside.

#### 4. **Acceptance: Short Pauses and Continuation**
- **What happens:** Shallow pullbacks, brief consolidations (1–5 days), price respects short-term moving averages.
- **Key trait:** Momentum is accepted, not discovered. Many pros add or scale positions here.

#### 5. **Trend Development: Flags, Wedges, Continuation Patterns**
- **What happens:** Sustained momentum with orderly pullbacks (bull flags, wedges, higher lows).
- **Key trait:** Momentum becomes more linear and persistent.

#### 6. **Maturation and Exhaustion**
- **What happens:** Volatility expands without progress, pullbacks deepen, continuation attempts fail.
- **Key trait:** Cycle ends; momentum transitions into distribution or range expansion without follow-through.

## 📌 Key Insights

### **Core Insight**
- Momentum is a **life cycle**, not a set of discrete strategies.
- Different traders enter at **different phases**, but all are trading the same underlying structure.

### ⏳ **Timing Over Tactics**
- Traders differ in **entry timing**, not in core principles.
- What matters most: **identifying which phase of the cycle price is in**.

### 📈 **Universal Principles of Momentum**
1. **Volatility contraction precedes expansion** — compression leads to explosive moves.
2. **Momentum expresses in bursts** — not all moves are equal; a few big trends drive most returns.
3. **Patience is required** — both before entry (waiting for the base) and after entry (letting the trend mature).
4. **Risk management varies by phase** — earlier phases have higher uncertainty, later phases offer better-defined risk.

### 👥 **Why Top Traders Succeed**
- They understand the **momentum life cycle**.
- They specialize in phases that match their **risk tolerance and timing preference**.
- They share the same **core principles** even if their rules differ.

### 📌 **Practical Implication for Traders**
- Instead of collecting “setups,” focus on **recognizing which phase of momentum you are in**.
- Adjust your **entry, position size, and risk management** based on the phase.
- Avoid trading exhaustion phases as continuation setups.
