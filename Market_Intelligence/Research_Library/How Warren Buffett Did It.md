# 🧠 AI Summary
> [cite_start]Warren Buffett is retiring at age 95 after a 75-year career, leaving a legacy as the world's most successful investor[cite: 514, 645]. [cite_start]He transformed Berkshire Hathaway from a failing textile company into a multi-billion dollar conglomerate by investing "float" from insurance operations into high-quality businesses[cite: 541, 542, 552]. [cite_start]His personal wealth, exceeding $125 billion, was built through disciplined value investing and the math of compounding[cite: 556, 565, 575]. [cite_start]Buffett has committed the majority of his fortune to charity, notably donating $31 billion to the Gates Foundation in 2006 and co-founding the Giving Pledge[cite: 721, 723].

## 📌 Key Insights
- [cite_start]**Core Philosophy:** Adhered to "value investing"—buying stocks as fractional ownership in businesses and holding long-term[cite: 565]. [cite_start]He famously stayed within his "circle of competence"[cite: 533].
- [cite_start]**Berkshire Hathaway Growth:** Acquired the textile firm in 1962 and became chairman in 1970[cite: 537, 541]. [cite_start]He utilized insurance "float" to fund acquisitions of companies like GEICO, BNSF Railway, and major stakes in Apple and Coca-Cola[cite: 543, 553].
- [cite_start]**Investment Traits:** Success is attributed to five traits: mental decisiveness, simplicity of thought, ability to distinguish quality, long-term focus, and strategic agility[cite: 586, 587, 588, 589, 591, 592].
- [cite_start]**The "Punch Card" Framework:** Recommended investors treat their career as having only 20 "punches" for new positions to enforce extreme discipline[cite: 611].
- [cite_start]**Public Persona:** Known as the "Oracle of Omaha," he turned annual shareholder meetings into the "Woodstock of Capitalism"[cite: 660, 682].
- [cite_start]**Philanthropic Legacy:** Shifted from compounding capital to massive giving after his wife Susan's death in 2004[cite: 718, 719, 720]. [cite_start]The Giving Pledge now has over 250 members[cite: 723].
