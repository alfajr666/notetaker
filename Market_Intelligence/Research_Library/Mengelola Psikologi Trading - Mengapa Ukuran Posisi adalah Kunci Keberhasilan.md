# 🧠 AI Summary
> [cite_start]The article argues that long-term success in the capital markets depends more on **self-control** and **position sizing** than on complex technical strategies[cite: 12, 13]. [cite_start]When traders take risks exceeding their mental endurance, the brain enters **"survival mode,"** where rational thought is replaced by impulsive emotional reactions like fear and panic[cite: 17, 18, 19]. [cite_start]Optimal position sizing—often perceived as "boring"—is the foundation for maintaining **objectivity and discipline**[cite: 32, 33, 35]. [cite_start]The core objective for small-capital traders should be **survival** through consistent processes rather than short-term gains[cite: 39, 40].

---

## 📌 Key Insights
* [cite_start]**The "Holy Grail" Fallacy**: Many traders fail by searching for a perfect technical strategy, ignoring that true success lies in emotional regulation[cite: 11, 12].
* [cite_start]**Survival Mode**: Excessive risk triggers the emotional centers of the brain, causing traders to abandon pre-set plans in favor of impulsive reactions[cite: 17, 18, 19].
* **Four Negative Behavioral Patterns**: Over-leveraging or oversized positions lead to:
    * [cite_start]**Hesitation**: Fear of entering the market despite valid signals[cite: 22].
    * [cite_start]**Shifting Stop Losses**: Inability to accept financial reality, leading to increased risk[cite: 28].
    * [cite_start]**Early Profit Taking**: Closing winning trades too soon due to anxiety over losing small gains[cite: 29, 30].
    * [cite_start]**Holding Losers**: Allowing losses to grow in hopes of a price reversal[cite: 31].
* [cite_start]**Psychological Freedom**: Smaller, controlled positions allow a trader to remain objective, treat market movements as impersonal, and focus on the **long-term average** of hundreds of trades[cite: 33, 34, 35, 37].
* [cite_start]**Definition of Success**: Success is measured by the **calmness and discipline** in executing a strategy, not the size of a single day's profit[cite: 41].
